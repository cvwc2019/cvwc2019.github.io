CVWC workshop Call for Papers

Overview

The goal of this workshop is to enhance the social responsibility of the CV community, encourage and highlight novel works to use CV techniques for wildlife conservation, and bring together researchers in the community to foster new ideas and directions on "CV for wildlife conservation".

Topics

Workshop topics include (but are not limited to):

  • Fine-grained wildlife recognition
  • Wildlife re-identification
  • Wildlife tracking
  • Smart trap sensor design
  • Drone based monitoring/tracking
  • Cloud visualization
  • Full conservation system
  • Dataset related to wildlife conservation
  • Challenge solutions

Submission

We invite paper submissions in two tracks:

  • Contributed papers: up to 6 pages, excluding references and acknowledgements. Submissions should use the ICCV template and be sent to the CVWC2019 CMT site before deadline (to be opened).
  • Challenge solution papers: up to 6 pages to describe the challenge solution, excluding references and acknowledgements. Submissions should use the ICCV template and be sent to the CVWC2019 CMT site before deadline (to be opened).

Contributed papers must contain novel work. They will receive at least two reviews with a single-blind policy. Papers will be selected based on novelty, relevance to workshop topics, technical merit. Accepted papers will be indexed on CVF and presented as posters in the workshop. Novel papers are selected to give oral presentation at the workshop.

Challenge solution papers will contain the full description of the team solution for any track of the challenge. Papers will be selected based on novelty and performance in the challenge. Accepted papers will be indexed on CVF and presented as posters in the workshop. Winner solutions are invited to give oral presentation at the workshop.

Important dates

  • Paper submission deadline: Aug 16, 2019
  • Notification to authors: Aug 23, 2019
  • Camera-ready deadline: Aug 30, 2019
  • Workshop date: Oct 27, 2019 (full day)
